Vision & Mission

VISION

Emerge as a centre for quality education in Electronics & Communication Engineering to create competent professionals.



MISSION

M1: Offer quality programs in Electronics & Communication Engineering aimed at fulfilling the needs of society and industry.
M2: Impart skills and develop scientific temper to solve complex technological problems of current times.
M3: Nurture ethics, talent, and entrepreneurship to inculcate professional development of students.

Under Graduate - Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)​

  • PEO1: Have in-depth knowledge in Electronics and Communication Engineering to innovate, design, and develop modern electronic systems.
  • PEO2: Sustain intellectual curiosity in professional career through life-long learning.
  • PEO3: Have strong work ethics, professional attitude, team spirit, leadership skills, and enterprising skills to serve industry and society.


Under Graduate - Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs)​​​

  • PSO1: Ability to analyze, design, and implement application specific electronic systems for complex engineering problems for analog and digital communications and signal processing applications by applying the knowledge of basic sciences, engineering mathematics and engineering fundamentals.
  • PSO2: Ability to adapt to rapid changes in tools and technology with an understanding of societal and ecological issues relevant to professional engineering practice through life-long learning.
  • PSO3: Excellent adaptability to function in a multi-disciplinary work environment, good interpersonal skills as a leader in a team in appreciation of professional ethics and societal responsibilities.


Post Graduate -Program Educational Objectives (PEOs)

  • PEO1: Identify and apply appropriate Electronic Design Automation software and electronic equipment to analyze, synthesize and evaluate to solve real-world problems in the VLSI domain to create innovative products and systems.
  • PEO2: Purse research studies in the core or allied areas of the VLSI circuits and systems.
  • PEO3: Inculcate positive attitude, professional ethics, effective communication, and interpersonal skills to succeed in the profession exhibiting creativity and innovation through research and development both as a team member and leader.


Post Graduate - Program Specific Outcomes (PSOs)

  • PSO1: Ability to design and integrate analog, digital, and mixed-signal circuits, and systems.
  • PSO2: Design and develop solutions for real-world problems in the domain of IC fabrication, testing, verification, and prototype development focusing on applications.
  • PSO3Understand, analyze, design, and simulate the various communication systems, data processing techniques and validate their performance for practical applications.

ECE Department Labs

As we believe that laboratory mirrors the knowledge and its experience will be a valuable part of engineering education, ECE Department has been designed to provide the students with the basic laboratory skills needed in engineering profession. The ability to build and evaluate the performance of electronics and communications equipment is essential for acquiring the knowledge in Electronics and Communications Engineering. Laboratory assignments are designed to emphasize the comprehensive skills on key concepts, rigorous analysis of electronic circuits and the presentation of results. Department Laboratorieswith focused research groups, are working in areas of communication and Electronics. The Department laboratory equipped with all kind of software’s and electronic systems to carry research relevant to the current technologies and needs of industry. To gain the experience from laboratory one must study each experiment and answer the pre-lab questions in advance of the laboratory meeting. A diligent effort to synchronize experiments with lecture topics has been made. students may be required at times to perform experiments on topics that have been fully covered in class. It is therefore important to be well prepared for each lab session by starting the pre-lab exercise early and asking questions as necessary. ​
